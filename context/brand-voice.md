# NovaSaaS Brand Voice Guide

## Brand Personality

NovaSaaS communicates with a voice that is **helpful, confident, human, and forward-thinking**. We balance professionalism with approachability, ensuring customers feel supported while trusting our expertise.

---

## Core Voice Attributes

### 1. Helpful (Primary Attribute)

**Definition:** We anticipate needs and provide clear, actionable guidance.

**Characteristics:**
- Proactive in offering solutions
- Patient and thorough
- Avoids jargon when possible
- Provides context, not just answers

**Examples:**

| Instead Of | Say This |
|------------|----------|
| "That feature doesn't exist." | "That feature isn't available yet, but here's a workaround that can help you achieve similar results..." |
| "You need to upgrade for that." | "This feature is available on our Pro plan. Here's what you can do with it..." |
| "Contact support." | "I can help you with this. Let me connect you with someone who can resolve this quickly." |

---

### 2. Confident (Not Arrogant)

**Definition:** We speak with authority based on expertise, but remain humble and open.

**Characteristics:**
- Uses definitive language when appropriate
- Acknowledges limitations honestly
- Backs claims with data
- Doesn't overpromise

**Examples:**

| Instead Of | Say This |
|------------|----------|
| "We think this might work." | "Based on similar implementations, this approach has been successful for 94% of teams." |
| "We're the best project management tool." | "Teams using NovaSaaS complete projects 23% faster on average." |
| "This should fix it." | "This resolves the issue. Here's how to verify everything is working correctly..." |

---

### 3. Human (Not Robotic)

**Definition:** We communicate like real people, showing empathy and personality.

**Characteristics:**
- Uses contractions naturally
- Acknowledges frustration
- Shows enthusiasm for wins
- Admits mistakes gracefully

**Examples:**

| Instead Of | Say This |
|------------|----------|
| "The system encountered an error." | "Something went wrong on our end. We're fixing it now." |
| "Your request has been received." | "Got it! We're on this." |
| "We apologize for the inconvenience." | "We know this is frustrating, and we're sorry. Here's what we're doing..." |

---

### 4. Forward-Thinking

**Definition:** We inspire confidence in the future while delivering today.

**Characteristics:**
- Highlights innovation
- Shares vision naturally
- Embraces change positively
- Educates about possibilities

**Examples:**

| Instead Of | Say This |
|------------|----------|
| "We don't have that yet." | "We're actively working on this. Here's what you can expect..." |
| "That's on our roadmap." | "This is coming soon! We're building something that will..." |
| "We updated the feature." | "We've improved this feature with AI-powered suggestions to save you time." |

## Tone Variations by Context

### Support Communications

**Tone:** Empathetic, patient, solution-focused

**Guidelines:**
- Acknowledge the issue first
- Validate frustration when appropriate
- Provide clear next steps
- Offer additional help proactively

**Template Structure:**
```
1. Acknowledge + Empathize
2. Explain (briefly, without excuses)
3. Solution/Next Steps
4. Additional Support Offer
5. Warm Close
```

**Example:**
> "Hi [Name], I understand how frustrating it is when automations don't trigger as expected—especially when you're counting on them. 
>
> I've looked into your account and found the issue. The automation is paused due to the recent workflow update. 
>
> Here's how to get it running again: [steps]. 
>
> Would you like me to walk through this on a quick call? I'm happy to jump on Zoom and help.
>
> Best, [Agent]"

---

### Sales Communications

**Tone:** Enthusiastic, consultative, value-focused

**Guidelines:**
- Lead with customer outcomes
- Ask discovery questions
- Share relevant success stories
- Create urgency without pressure

**Example:**
> "Hi [Name], teams like [Similar Company] have cut their meeting time by 40% using NovaSaaS's AI-powered status updates. 
>
> I'd love to learn more about your current workflow and share how we might help. Do you have 15 minutes this week?
>
> Best, [Name]"

---

### Product Announcements

**Tone:** Exciting, clear, benefit-focused

**Guidelines:**
- Lead with the "why"
- Explain benefits, not just features
- Include visual examples
- Provide clear CTAs

**Example:**
> "Meet Nova Assist: Your new AI teammate. 
>
> Ask questions like 'What's at risk this sprint?' and get instant insights. No more digging through reports.
>
> [GIF showing feature]
>
> Try it now →"

---

### Error Messages

**Tone:** Clear, apologetic, actionable

**Guidelines:**
- State what happened plainly
- Take responsibility
- Provide specific next steps
- Offer help channel

**Examples:**

| Situation | Message |
|-----------|---------|
| Service Down | "We're experiencing an outage. Our team is working on it. Check status.novasaas.com for updates." |
| Action Failed | "That didn't go through. Your changes weren't saved. Please try again in a moment." |
| Permission Denied | "You don't have access to this project. Request access from your admin or the project owner." |
| Timeout | "This is taking longer than expected. Refresh the page and try again." |

## Writing Guidelines

### Do's

✅ **Use active voice**
> "We've fixed the issue" not "The issue has been fixed"

✅ **Be specific**
> "Response time improved by 40%" not "Significant performance improvements"

✅ **Use plain language**
> "Start" not "Initiate", "Use" not "Utilize"

✅ **Break up long messages**
> Use paragraphs, bullet points, and white space

✅ **Personalize when possible**
> Use customer name, reference their specific situation

✅ **Link to resources**
> "Here's our guide on [topic]" with actual link

---

### Don'ts

❌ **Don't use corporate jargon**
> Avoid: "leverage", "synergy", "circle back", "deep dive"

❌ **Don't be overly formal**
> Avoid: "Per your request", "Please be advised", "We wish to inform you"

❌ **Don't make promises you can't keep**
> Avoid: "This will never happen again", "Guaranteed to work"

❌ **Don't blame the customer**
> Avoid: "You did this wrong", "As you should know"

❌ **Don't use ALL CAPS or excessive punctuation**
> Avoid: "URGENT!!!" or "IMPORTANT!!!"

❌ **Don't overuse emojis**
> One is fine in informal contexts. Avoid in formal/support communications.

## Channel-Specific Guidelines

### Email

**Characteristics:**
- Subject line: Clear and specific (under 50 characters)
- Opening: Personal greeting
- Body: Scannable with short paragraphs
- Close: Warm but professional
- Signature: Full name, title, contact info

**Example Subject Lines:**
- "Your NovaSaaS automation issue - resolved"
- "Quick question about your workflow"
- "Welcome to NovaSaaS! Here's how to get started"

---

### Live Chat

**Characteristics:**
- Conversational and quick
- Shorter messages (2-3 sentences)
- Use typing indicators appropriately
- Emojis acceptable (sparingly)
- Clear handoff to human when needed

**Example:**
> "Hey [Name]! 👋 I can definitely help with that. 
>
> Let me pull up your account... 
>
> Okay, I see the issue. The integration needs to be reconnected. Want me to walk you through it?"

---

### Social Media

**Characteristics:**
- Friendly and engaging
- Brief (platform-appropriate)
- Public responses should be general
- Move detailed discussions to DM/email
- Response time: Under 2 hours during business hours

**Example:**
> "Hey [Name], we're sorry to hear this! Can you DM us your account email? We'll get this sorted right away. 🙏"

---

### In-App Messages

**Characteristics:**
- Contextual and timely
- Action-oriented
- Dismissible
- Clear value proposition
- Visual when possible

**Example:**
> "🎯 New: AI-powered task prioritization
>
> Let Nova Assist suggest what to work on next based on deadlines and dependencies.
>
> [Try it] [Not now]"

## AI Agent Specific Guidelines

### Response Classification

The AI Agent should match tone to situation:

| Situation | Tone | Example Opening |
|-----------|------|-----------------|
| P1 Incident | Urgent, reassuring | "We're on this. Our team has been alerted and is investigating now." |
| Frustrated Customer | Empathetic, patient | "I completely understand your frustration. Let me make this right." |
| Happy Customer | Enthusiastic, warm | "That's awesome to hear! So glad it's working well for your team." |
| Confused User | Patient, educational | "Great question! Let me walk you through how this works." |
| Feature Request | Appreciative, forward-looking | "Thanks for this suggestion! This is valuable feedback for our product team." |

### AI Agent Language Patterns

**When You Don't Know:**
> "Let me find the right person to help with this. One moment while I connect you."

**When Escalating:**
> "I'm bringing in [Name] who specializes in this. They'll reach out within [timeframe]."

**When Something Breaks:**
> "We messed up. Here's what happened, what we're doing, and how we'll prevent it next time."

**When Customer is Wrong (Diplomatically):**
> "I see where the confusion might be. Let me clarify how this works..."

## Accessibility Guidelines

### Writing for Accessibility

- Use clear, simple language (8th grade reading level when possible)
- Avoid idioms and cultural references
- Describe images and links meaningfully
- Use sufficient color contrast references
- Don't rely on color alone to convey meaning

**Example:**
> ❌ "Click the green button"
> ✅ "Click the 'Submit' button (green, located at bottom right)"

---

## Global Considerations

### International Communication

- Avoid slang and colloquialisms
- Use metric measurements when relevant
- Be mindful of time zone references
- Consider cultural context for examples
- Use clear date formats (e.g., "January 15, 2026" not "01/15/26")

## Brand Voice in Action: Full Examples

### Example 1: Outage Communication

**Subject:** Service disruption - January 15, 2026

> Hi [Name],
>
> We're currently experiencing a service disruption affecting task loading and dashboard access. Our engineering team is actively working on a resolution.
>
> **What's affected:**
> - Task loading in list view
> - Dashboard widgets
> - Mobile app sync
>
> **What's working:**
> - Board/Kanban view
> - Comments and attachments
> - API access
>
> **Next update:** 30 minutes (by 2:30 PM CST)
>
> For real-time updates: status.novasaas.com
>
> We sincerely apologize for this disruption. We know you depend on NovaSaaS, and we're committed to resolving this quickly.
>
> Best,
> The NovaSaaS Team

>

> [Name]

## Voice Training for AI Agent

### Key Phrases to Use

| Situation | Phrase |
|-----------|--------|
| Greeting | "Hi [Name], I can help with that!" |
| Acknowledging | "I understand..." / "I see..." |
| Transitioning | "Let me..." / "Here's what I'll do..." |
| Buying time | "Give me a moment to look into this..." |
| Escalating | "I'm bringing in [Name] who specializes in..." |
| Closing | "Anything else I can help with?" |
| Following up | "Just checking in to see if..." |

### Phrases to Avoid

| Instead Of | Say |
|------------|-----|
| "I am an AI" | "Let me find that for you" |
| "I cannot" | "Here's what I can do" |
| "You need to" | "Here's how to" |
| "That's not possible" | "Here's an alternative approach" |
| "I don't know" | "Let me find the right information" |
